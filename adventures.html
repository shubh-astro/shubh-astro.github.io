ADVENTURES & REFLECTIONS

A journal of my time in the field, treks through challenging terrain, and explorations of hobbies, from capturing the night sky to mechanical keyboards. This is where I share my perspective outside of core research.

M42: Capturing the Heart of Orion

February 2024 | Vattiyoorkavu Observatory, Kerala

This image is a stack of 40 x 30s exposures of the Orion Nebula (M42), a massive star-forming region. I processed the data in PixInsight and Photoshop, focusing on bringing out the delicate wisps of dust and the faint outer hydrogen-alpha regions without completely blowing out the bright stellar core. The long drive and late nights were certainly worth this result!

The Calm Before the Campaign

October 2023 | Hanle, Ladakh

Setting up the high-resolution spectrograph at the Indian Astronomical Observatory. The air is incredibly thin and the cold is biting, but the sheer clarity of the sky at 4,500m is unmatched. The challenge here is less about the stars and more about keeping the equipment operational at these extreme altitudes—a true test of engineering!

The 'Cosmic Dust' Custom Build

June 2023 | Project completion

Taking a necessary break from coding to build a custom mechanical keyboard. This one is a 65% layout with deep blue keycaps to mimic the night sky and Gateron Black switches for that satisfying, quiet 'thock'. It's the perfect blend of engineering and aesthetic precision, and it makes those long night coding sessions much more pleasant.

Conquering the Deoriatal-Chandrashila Trek

May 2024 | Uttarakhand, Himalayas

There is nothing quite like the view of the Chaukhamba massif reflecting in the Deoriatal lake at sunrise. This three-day trek was a perfect reminder of the raw, untamed beauty of my home state. It recharges the mind and provides the perspective needed for intense research periods—a great analogy for life and work.
